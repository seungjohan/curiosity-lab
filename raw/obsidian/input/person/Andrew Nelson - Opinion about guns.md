### What we discussed
- HIs opinion
Opinion on guns is that they're cool, but at the same time a dangerous weapon. They need to be handled responsibly but are a lot of fun to shoot. I only have mine for recreational reasons and as a collectors thing, so just fun at the range. I could use them for self defense if someone ever broke into my house but I don't anticipate ever needing to do that. 

As for gun laws I think they were made by people who don't really know much about Firearms and are pointlessly prohibitive without actually doing anything to decrease crime. I get that people want to do something because it feels like mass shootings are always in headlines but almost none of the ideas they come up with will actually do anything effective because they aren't educated. #1 cause of firearm deaths in America is suicide. Pistols kill a lot more people than rifles. Actually more people are beaten to deaths with fists or feet or whatever than are killed with rifles. Most gun related deaths outside of suicide are gang related, usually with illegally obtained Firearms. For a little while murders were decreasing but looks like at least for 2021 they spiked, I'm assuming because of the pandemic, unsure of what it looks like after that year. So things like trying to ban "assault rifles" or high capacity magazines or whatever won't do anything to reduce crime because it's already a tiny subsection of deaths and just turns a bunch of law abiding citizens into criminals for no real gain. Also we already have background checks on gun sales, anyone with an FFL (I believe it stands for federal firearms license, it's some sort of license to sell guns) is required by law to run a background check on someone and will refuse to sell to people who fail it. That's not to say I don't think gun control should exist, I just think we should focus more on who we prevent from owning guns and being much stricter on crimes in general rather than on the guns themselves. We could also do more in educating people to be more responsible gun owners too, seems like a decent amount of shootings are done by kids accessing their parent's firearms so improving the way we store them.

As for the nra I don't have any opinion on them. I know it's a massive government lobbying thing but their reputation even within the gun owner community has gone down a lot. I forget the reasons for it but I know a lot of people are saying to donate to other organizations now.
