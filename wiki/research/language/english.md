# English Vocabulary

| Word | Meaning | Definition | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Conceit** | Excessive Pride | An exaggerated, overly high opinion of oneself, one's abilities, or one's appearance. Synonymous with vanity, egotism, or arrogance. | "His conceit made it difficult for him to accept feedback." |
| | Literary/Artistic Device | An elaborate, fanciful, or far-fetched metaphor. Can also refer to the overarching, clever organizing premise of a plot or work. | "The film's central conceit hinges on a time-loop that forces the protagonist to relive the same day." |
| **fizzle out** | Fading conclusion | Started but loses steam, ending anti-climactically. (KR: **흐지부지되다**) | "My plans keep fizzling out before I finish them." |
| **peter out** | Gradual weakening | Gradually weakens, diminishes, or tapers off. (KR: **흐지부지되다**) | "The project kept petering out after a strong start." |
| **instill** | Gradually introduce | To gradually but firmly establish an idea, attitude, or habit in a person's mind. (KR: **스며들게 하다 / 심어주다**) | "It is important to instill a love of learning in children." |
| **prestige** | High status / Reputation | Widespread respect and admiration felt for someone or something on the basis of a perception of their achievements or quality. | "The university has a great deal of prestige." |
| **unleash** | Release powerful force | Cause a strong or violent force to be released or become unrestrained. (KR: **촉발하다 / 해방시키다**) | "The new policy unleashed a wave of protest across the city." |
