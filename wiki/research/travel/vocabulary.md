# ✈️ Travel Vocabulary

This page consolidates travel-related terms across different languages and categories.

## 🏨 Accommodation
| Word | Meaning | Example | etc |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ryokan (旅館)** | A traditional Japanese inn featuring tatami-matted rooms and communal baths. | "Staying at a Ryokan in Hakone is a classic experience." | JP: 旅館 |
| **Parador** | A luxury hotel in Spain or Puerto Rico, usually located in a historic building like a castle or monastery. | "The Parador de Granada is located within the Alhambra grounds." | ES: Parador |
| **Pousada** | A traditional Portuguese luxury hotel, often situated in a historical building. | "We stayed in a converted convent Pousada in Évora." | PT: Pousada |

## 🚆 Transport & Logistics
| Word | Meaning | Example | etc |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Layover** | A short stay in a place in between parts of a journey. | "We have a four-hour layover in Singapore." | |
| **Open-jaw** | A round-trip ticket where the traveler returns from a different city than the one they arrived in. | "We booked an open-jaw flight: arriving in London and departing from Paris." | |
| **Tuk-tuk** | A motorized three-wheeled taxi, common in many parts of Southeast Asia. | "We took a tuk-tuk to explore the night market in Bangkok." | |

## 🗺️ Regional Terms
| Word | Meaning | Example | etc |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Autobahn** | The federal highway system in Germany, famous for having no federally mandated speed limit on some sections. | "Driving on the Autobahn requires high concentration." | DE: Autobahn |
| **Zócalo** | The main public square in many Mexican cities. | "The Zócalo in Mexico City is one of the largest squares in the world." | MX: Zócalo |
| **Caleta** | A small cove or inlet, often used in Spanish-speaking coastal regions. | "We found a beautiful hidden caleta for swimming." | ES: Caleta |
