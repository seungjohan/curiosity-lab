> [!IMPORTANT] Key Takeaway (Product & Creative Perspective)
> **Why this matters:** Focaccia is a high-hydration, "low-friction" bread. It prioritizes surface area for flavor delivery.
> **How to use it:** The "Dimpling" technique creates functional pockets for salt and oil—think of it as designing "receptors" for user-added value (toppings).
> **Informs:** Surface design and accessibility.

# 🥯 Focaccia

A flat leavened Italian oven-baked bread, similar in style and texture to pizza dough.

## 🥣 Key Features
- **High Hydration**: Usually 70-80% water to flour, resulting in a soft, airy crumb.
- **Dimpling**: Using fingers to create indentations in the dough to prevent it from rising too high and to hold olive oil.
- **Brine (Samoia)**: A mixture of water, olive oil, and salt poured over the dough before baking for maximum moisture and flavor.
