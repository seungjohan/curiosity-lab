> [!IMPORTANT] Key Takeaway
> **Why this matters:** Coq au Vin is a masterclass in braising and flavor extraction, demonstrating how a "tough" ingredient (traditionally an old rooster) can be transformed into a luxury product through time and the right medium (wine).
> **How to use it:** Focus on the "Fond" and "Deglazing" steps to build the sauce's structural depth.
> **Informs:** Process optimization and iterative improvement of a core base.

# 🐓 Coq au Vin

Coq au Vin is a classic French stew where chicken is braised with red wine (traditionally Burgundy), lardons (salty pork fat), mushrooms, and onions. It is a quintessential example of French country cooking that relies on high-quality ingredients and patient technique.

## 🥘 Components & Technique
- **The Bird:** Traditionally made with an old rooster (coq), which required a long braise in acidic wine to tenderize. Modern versions use standard chicken.
- **The Marinade:** Often marinated overnight in red wine with aromatics (bouquet garni) to penetrate the meat.
- **Lardons:** Provide the essential fat base and a salty, smoky depth.
- **The Sauce:** Thickened either by reduction or a roux/beurre manié, the sauce should be glossy and deep burgundy in color.
