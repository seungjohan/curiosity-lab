# La Cime

Who, what, when, where: Located in central Osaka’s business district, Hommachi, La Cime showcases the impeccable modern French cuisine of chef Yusuke Takada. Having trained in Lyon and worked in Paris at renowned eateries Le Taillevent and Le Meurice, Takada opened La Cime as his own venture in 2010. What’s on the plate? Takada’s menu is elaborate, precise, creative, beautifully executed and unfailingly tasty. Along with premium seafood and produce from western Japan, don’t be surprised if you also find citrus and other produce that reflect his origins from subtropical Amami Oshima, a small island in the far south of the country. The signature dish, the Boudin Dog, a batter-fried morsel of boudin noir (blood sausage), coloured with edible bamboo and charcoal eaten in a single bite, cannot be missed. Dramatic dining: The walls and ceilings of the stylish and minimalist dining room are painted black, with black chairs too, accented by crisp white tablecloths and warm wooden floors.
