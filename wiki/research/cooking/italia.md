## 🍝 Pasta
- **Fresh Pasta**: Pasta made with fresh dough, typically containing eggs and flour, known for its soft, tender texture and shorter cooking time compared to dried pasta.
- **Semolina**: Coarse, purified wheat middlings of durum wheat; traditionally used to make dried pasta or bread, giving it a firmer texture.
- **Fettuccine**: Flat, thick pasta made of egg and flour (usually common in Roman and Tuscan cuisine).
- **Linguine**: Long, thin, flat strands of pasta, similar to spaghetti but flattened; traditionally served with seafood.
- **Orecchiette**: Small, ear-shaped pasta originating from Puglia, with a textured surface perfect for holding sauce.

## 🥫 Sauces
- **Ragù**: A rich, meat-based sauce typically slow-cooked and served with pasta (e.g., "Ragù alla Bolognese").
- **Pelati**: Peeled tomatoes, typically high-quality canned plum tomatoes (like San Marzano).
- **San Marzano Pomodoro**: The gold standard for Italian tomatoes, grown in Sarno valley volcanic soil near Mount Vesuvius. Essential for authentic *Pizza Napoletana*.
