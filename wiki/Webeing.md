# Webeing

## Overview
A B2B2C food e-commerce platform (2020.01 - 2021.06) focused on reducing food waste by selling near-expiry or surplus items (especially fruits and side dishes) at a discount.

## Retrospective

### Reasons for Closing
1.  **Role Ambiguity**: Lack of clear role definitions within the founding team.
2.  **Pivot Failure**: Incorrect timing or direction of pivots.
3.  **Partner Issues**: Starting with fruit shops was a challenge; should have started with a more agile MVP.
4.  **Development Struggles**: Tech stack and dev speed were insufficient for the business needs.
5.  **Small Market**: While the F&B market is huge, the specific niche targeted was harder to scale than anticipated.

### Key Learnings
- **MVP Strategy**: Focus on the smallest possible product to test the most critical hypothesis.
- **Customer Acquisition**: Traditional marketing (flyers) was less effective than targeted community marketing (Mom cafes, apartment complexes).
- **Product Roadmap**: Conflicts over UI/UX (e.g., Map vs. List) should be resolved with user feedback, not internal debate.

## Historical Context
- **Team**: 2 founders, 1 designer, 4 engineers.
- **Awards**: Korea Gov 'Preliminary Founder' package, Lotte & Likelion Hackathon award.
