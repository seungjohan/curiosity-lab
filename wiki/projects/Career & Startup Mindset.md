# Career & Startup Mindset

## Transitioning from Founder
- **The "Startup High" (Rush)**: It's easy to get trapped in the identity of being a "former founder." This can lead to self-justification and a refusal to accept the current reality of being a job seeker.
- **Closing the Chapter**: To open a new career chapter, the old one (startup) must be mentally closed. Using the past as a shield against current failures is counterproductive.
- **Self-Worth**: Career status shouldn't define total self-worth. Rebuilding confidence starts with small wins and stable employment.

## Professional Conduct
- **Critique with Solutions**: Being critical of others' ideas without offering solutions is "rude," not "visionary." 
- **Acknowledging Shortcomings**: Be honest about why a startup failed (tech debt, market size, team roles) instead of romanticizing the struggle.
