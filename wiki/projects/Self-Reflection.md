> **Informs:** Personal leadership style and social interactions.

# Self-Reflection

## Personality & Growth
- **Stubbornness vs. Leadership**: A recurring question: "Is my leadership actually just stubbornness?" Need to distinguish between being firm and being closed-minded.
- **The "Honesty" Trap**: Avoid masking rudeness as honesty. Critical feedback should be constructive and empathetic, not just sharp.
- **Perfectionism**: "Done is better than perfect." Avoid delaying projects (like videos or music) because the quality isn't "perfect" yet. Completion is the goal.
- **Self-Judgment**: The realization that judging others often stems from self-insecurity (defense mechanism). To like others, one must first like oneself.

## The Frame of Reference
- **Subjective Reality**: Everyone sees the world through a different frame. My frame is not the absolute truth; it's just one way of living.
- **Anti-Isolation**: Staying at home too long distorts self-perception. Interaction and movement (like morning runs) are essential to stay grounded in reality.

## Key Insights
- **Liking People First**: Popularity and good relationships come to those whose "list of people I like" is the longest.
- **The Power of Small Talk**: It's not just "noise"—it's a way to generate positive energy and connect with the world.
